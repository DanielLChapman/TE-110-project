VIEWS OF TECHNOLOGY

Standard View

This is the "standard" view, a "commonsense view". Mimics everyday Understanding

  • Does: This view states that all technology happens because of a necessity. If there is a need for the technology in society, then it will be created.
  • Does not: Standard view does not deny that technology is a human endeavor, technology is the end result to a problem
  • Believes: Technology is a tool that empowers societies and peoples to spread their voice and knowledge.

Sleep Walking view

This is the view that we do not realize or find the importance of studying how much technology effects us

  • Does: Deny how much technology effects our everyday lives.
  • Does not: Does not study or give thought to the long term implications of our technology use.
  • Believes: That technology is just a tool to use when needed.

Technological Determinism

This is the view that technology is a powerful tool that dictates the patterns of human social and cultural life

  • Does: Believe that technology drives the development of a society.
  • Does: Gives weight to the idea that a belief in determinism is linked to a belief in technological autonomy
  • Believes: That technology is out of control and we can not step back and examine the effects on our society.

Technological Utopianism

This is the view that believes technology is the key to utopia.

  • Does: Believe that technology is a savior
  • Does: Worship technology to a religious level.
  • Believes: That a techno-utopia is possible and would solve the worlds problems, including death.

Luddism and Neo-Luddism

This is the view that the followers resist technological change

  • Believes: Technology destroys the way of life
  • Does: Strive to abolish the spread of technology
  • Believes: That technology is expanding at a rate that it will be humans undoing.

Fetishism & Anthropological

Fetishism: This is the view that technology is a superior power, godlike

Anthropological: This is the view that technology cant be studied outside of culture contexts.

  • Fetishism Believes: Technology effects humans in ways that cannot be understood.
  • Anthropological Believes: The study of technology is more how it affects cultures and economies rather than how it is used.
